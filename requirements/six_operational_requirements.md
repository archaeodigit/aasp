# Six operational requirements for epistemic revisability

1. **Preserve evidence states.** Records generated under different procedures, seasons, or documentation stages remain separately addressable.
2. **Preserve claim lineage.** Later assertions are linked to earlier claims with a relation type, basis, direction, and reason.
3. **Make selection explicit.** Inclusion and exclusion decisions identify their target, status, criterion, and reason.
4. **Separate reusable plans from concrete executions.** A declared analytical view remains distinct from each run that applies it.
5. **Compare synthesis states.** Assertions produced under different evidence, procedures, criteria, or releases can be retrieved side by side.
6. **Distinguish publication change from scholarly revision.** Technical republication is separated from changed archaeological interpretation.
